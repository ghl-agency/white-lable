# Branding and Customization – March 2026

Agencies presenting software tools to clients under a third-party brand face a credibility problem. Clients see the vendor name, not the agency. This creates dependency confusion and undermines the agency's perceived value. GoHighLevel White Label resolves this by allowing agencies to deploy the entire platform under their own brand through the [fully branded agency CRM](https://www.gohighlevel.com/?fp_ref=smetrix28), with no GoHighLevel branding visible to clients at any level.

---

## What Branding and Customization Controls

The branding system applies agency identity across every client-facing element of the platform. Configuration is handled from the agency's master account and propagates across all sub-accounts automatically.

- Apply a custom agency logo across the dashboard, login screen, and client portal
- Set a custom domain so clients access the platform through the agency's own URL
- Configure brand colors throughout the interface
- Replace all GoHighLevel references with the agency's product name
- Customize the client-facing email sender name and address
- Set custom favicon and browser tab identity

---

## Custom Domain Configuration

The platform supports full white label domain mapping. Agencies point their own subdomain to the GoHighLevel infrastructure. Clients interact exclusively with the agency-branded URL. No GoHighLevel domain appears in the browser, in emails, or in any client-facing communication.

This configuration applies to the client login portal, the dashboard environment, and all automated email communications generated through [Marketing Automation and Workflows](marketing-automation).

---

## Brand Consistency Across Modules

Branding settings apply uniformly across the entire platform. When a client accesses their account, every module they interact with carries the agency's identity.

This includes the [CRM and Pipeline Management](crm-pipelines) interface, the [Funnel and Landing Page Builder](funnels-landing-pages), the [Appointment Scheduling System](appointment-scheduling), the [Reputation Management Tools](reputation-management), and the [Reporting and Analytics](reporting-analytics) dashboard. The [Social Planner](social-planner) and [AI Agents](ai-agents) modules operate under the same branded environment.

---

## Client Perception and Agency Positioning

When clients log into a platform that carries the agency's name, logo, and domain, the agency is positioned as the software provider. This increases perceived value, reduces client churn, and supports premium pricing.

The branding layer also applies to client onboarding communications. Automated sequences managed through [Client Management](client-management) and billing notifications generated through [Billing and Pricing Management](billing-pricing) all carry the agency brand.

---

## Administrative Branding Controls

The agency's master account controls all branding settings. Sub-account users have no access to branding configuration. This separation is enforced through the permission structure documented in [Team and Permission Controls](team-permissions).

Agencies integrating third-party tools through [Integrations and API Capabilities](integrations) maintain the same branded environment across connected platforms where supported.
