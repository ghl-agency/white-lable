# Marketing Automation and Workflows – March 2026

Agencies delivering marketing services across multiple client accounts cannot scale manual campaign execution. Sending individual emails, following up with leads by hand, and managing multi-step sequences without automation creates a ceiling on how many clients the agency can serve effectively. GoHighLevel White Label removes that ceiling through a native workflow engine that automates multi-channel campaigns across all client accounts from one environment, available through the [agency marketing automation platform](https://www.gohighlevel.com/?fp_ref=smetrix28).

---

## Workflow Builder

The workflow builder creates automated sequences triggered by contact actions, time delays, or external events. Each workflow operates independently within its assigned client sub-account as configured through [Client Management](client-management).

- Build multi-step sequences across email, SMS, voice, and voicemail drop
- Set triggers based on form submissions, tag assignments, pipeline stage changes, or appointment bookings
- Apply conditional logic to branch sequences based on contact behavior
- Use time delays, wait conditions, and scheduled send windows
- Clone workflows across client accounts without rebuilding

---

## Campaign Channels

Workflows execute across multiple communication channels from a single sequence. Agencies configure the channel mix based on client industry and contact preferences.

- Email campaigns with custom templates and dynamic personalization fields
- SMS sequences with two-way reply handling
- Outbound voice drops delivered to contact voicemail
- In-app notifications for internal team actions
- Webhook triggers for connections to external platforms managed through [Integrations and API Capabilities](integrations)

---

## Lead Nurture and Follow-Up

Automated follow-up sequences activate immediately when a new lead enters the system through a form, funnel, or chat interaction. The workflow executes the follow-up sequence without manual initiation.

Lead entry points include forms built in the [Funnel and Landing Page Builder](funnels-landing-pages), conversations initiated through [AI Agents](ai-agents), and appointments booked through the [Appointment Scheduling System](appointment-scheduling). Contact records created through these entry points feed directly into [CRM and Pipeline Management](crm-pipelines) and trigger the assigned automation sequence.

---

## Review and Reputation Workflows

Automated sequences support the review collection process documented in [Reputation Management Tools](reputation-management). After a completed appointment or closed transaction, the workflow sends a review request through the configured channel without requiring manual follow-up.

---

## Reporting and Performance

Workflow performance data is available in the dashboards documented in [Reporting and Analytics](reporting-analytics). Agencies track open rates, reply rates, conversion events, and sequence completion rates per client account.

---

## Access and Configuration Controls

Workflow creation and editing permissions are assigned through the role structure in [Team and Permission Controls](team-permissions). Client sub-account users can be restricted from modifying automations the agency has configured. All automated communications carry the agency brand established in [Branding and Customization](branding-customization). Client subscription status managed in [Billing and Pricing Management](billing-pricing) can serve as a workflow trigger. Campaign content can be coordinated with scheduled social posts managed through the [Social Planner](social-planner).
